ॐ Rite of Offering Ankle Bells ॐ

Kuchipudi - a classical dance form originating from the village of Kuchipudi in Andhra Pradesh, is deeply rooted in the ancient Hindu Sanskrit text of Natya Shastra, developed as a religious art linked to traveling bards, temples, and spiritual beliefs.

Gajja Pooja is a traditional ceremony that reinforces the sacred Guru-Shishya Parampara, wherein students receive their ankle bells (gajjalu) from their guru —an emblem of their readiness to embrace spiritual discipline and advance in their dance journey.

It signifies a profound milestone, rooted in discipline, devotion, and dedication.

Today marks the first invocatory performance of these shishyas with their ankle bells—an offering to both the divine and the art form of Kuchipudi.

Guru Smt. Prafulla Velury

“Natyasudhalahari” Smt. Prafulla Velury is a distinguished Kuchipudi dancer, choreographer, nattuvannar, and accomplished Carnatic singer. Smt. Prafulla Velury's journey in Kuchipudi, began under the guidance of Guru Late Sri Yeleswarapu Suryaprakash Sharma.
She continued her training with Guru “Nataraja Hamsa” Sri D.V. Satyakumar, a disciple of the legendary Guru Late. Dr. Vempati Chinna Satyam, who played a pivotal role in modernizing and popularizing Kuchipudi.

A graduate of Telugu University with a major in Kuchipudi dance, Smt. Prafulla initially trained in Carnatic vocal under her mother, Smt. Bachu Sarojini Devi, and is currently advancing her learning with Vidwan Sri. Vittal Ramamurthy.
Renowned for her energetic and loving teaching style, Smt. Prafulla epitomizes the “Guru-Shishya” parampara, a tradition that has preserved the purity of Indian classical dance since Vedic times. This tradition emphasizes the deep bond between teacher and student, ensuring the transmission of knowledge, technique, and cultural values across generations.
